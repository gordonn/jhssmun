World Health Organization

Access to Reproductive Healthcare

COMMITTEE OVERVIEW

Enter the World Health Organization (WHO), a global authority on international public health established in 1948. Headquartered in Geneva, Switzerland, the WHO aims to improve global health through six key priorities, including universal health coverage and addressing social determinants of health. The committee, suitable for all experience levels, offers a traditional Model UN experience.

In this session, the WHO committee focuses on access to reproductive healthcare. Delegates tackle challenges like gender inequality, social stigma, economic disparities, and legal restrictions. The committee encourages exploration of the complex interplay of cultural, economic, and political factors affecting healthcare accessibility. Delegates are expected to approach discussions with a commitment to human rights, gender equality, and overall community health, contributing to inclusive and effective global policies.

Further inquiries regarding this committee, position papers, and general questions can be submitted to jhssmunwho@gmail.com.
