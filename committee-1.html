United Nations Security Council

Border Disputes in the South China Sea

COMMITTEE OVERVIEW

The UN Security Council (UNSC) gathers to tackle the South China Sea border disputes, a pivotal issue with far-reaching geopolitical consequences. Delegates will scrutinize territorial claims, maritime rights, and regional security concerns. The committee aims to formulate resolutions that promote peaceful negotiations, uphold international law, and safeguard the rights of affected nations. Delegates must navigate complex diplomatic terrain, fostering dialogue among involved parties while ensuring stability in this strategically significant region. The UNSC serves as the global stage for cooperative efforts, urging member states to collaboratively address and resolve the South China Sea border disputes.

Further inquiries regarding this committee, position papers, and general questions can be submitted to jhssmununsc@gmail.com.
